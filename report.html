Key Insights

XFA vs AcroForm: 5,179 of 6,973 unmatched fields (74%) use generic XFA names like TextField1[0] that cannot be matched by field-name pattern matching. Excluding XFA, the composition engine covers 57% of descriptive AcroForm fields.
Strongest patterns: Signature (90 forms), Address (65 forms), SSN (57 forms), Date (54 forms), and Full Name (53 forms) are the most widely applicable patterns across VA forms.
Gap: XFA forms need OCR-like approach. The 22 forms with 0% coverage are mostly XFA-based with generic field names. For these, a layout-based or label-proximity approach would be needed rather than field-name matching.
228 VBA forms pending: Forms hosted on www.vba.va.gov (including high-value benefits forms like 21-526EZ, 21-0966) were blocked by cross-origin restrictions. These are primarily fillable "ARE" (AcroForm-enabled) PDFs and likely have strong pattern coverage. A server-side proxy or VBA-domain analysis pass is recommended.

Methodology

This analysis was performed by downloading each PDF from the VA Forms API, loading it with pdf-lib, extracting all AcroForm field names, and matching each against 17 regex-based composition patterns (SSN, DOB, Full Name, Address, Phone, Email, Date, Signature, Checkbox, Radio, VA File Number, Service Number, Insurance, Medical, Financial, Dependent, Employer). Forms hosted on www.va.gov (437 forms) were analyzed directly; www.vba.va.gov forms (228) and other-domain forms (21) require a separate analysis pass due to cross-origin restrictions. Forms that timed out after 8 seconds were recorded as errors. Forms with 0 AcroForm fields are classified as flat/scanned PDFs.
